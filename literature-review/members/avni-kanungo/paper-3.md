# 📄 Paper 3: An integrated encyclopedia of DNA elements in the human genome
*   **Journal / Conference**: Nature (2012)
*   **DOI**: `10.1038/nature11247`
*   **Link**: [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature11247)

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _The human genome sequence provides the fundamental code for human biology, but the specific functions of the vast majority of its nearly three billion bases._ |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _The ENCODE project produced 1,640 datasets across 147 cell types to systematically map transcription, transcription factor association, chromatin structure, and DNA methylation using advanced sequencing assays like RNA-seq, ChIP-seq, and DNase-seq. The researchers then integrated these diverse datasets to define discrete functional elements and classify the genome into distinct chromatin states_ |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _The project revealed that 80.4% of the human genome participates in at least one biochemical event, with 99% of the genome located within 1.7 kb of a measured biochemical event. Furthermore, the study demonstrated that non-coding sequence variants linked to human diseases by genome-wide association studies (GWAS) are significantly enriched within these newly identified functional regulatory elements._ |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _Two major limitations wereidentified: (i) As the project only analysed a fraction of known transcription factors and sampled a limited number of specialized or developmentally restricted cell lineages, the identified proportion of functional bases is considered an underestimate. (ii) The regional classifications are limited by the specific assays used and likely contain heterogeneous classes of elements that require further experimental validation to fully understand their mechanistic processes._ |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _Future initiatives must expand this foundational resource by profiling additional transcription factors, chromatin modifications, and diverse cell types. A major subsequent goal is to use this functional data to assign a potential impact on human phenotypes to every single genomic variant._ |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _ENCODE's comprehensive annotations justify the use of whole-genome sequencing over exome-only sequencing, as they provide the necessary framework to interpret the functional impact of somatic and rare variants located in the non-coding regions of the genome._ |
