## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _The exponential growth of complex multimodal brain imaging and advanced genomics data creates massive computational and statistical hurdles for researchers mapping the genetic foundations of brain function and disorders._ |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _This paper present a comprehensive review of machine learning and statistical methods applied to brain imaging genomics. The evaluated frameworks encompass heritability estimation, algorithms for discovering complex multi-SNP and multi-trait associations (such as multivariate regression and sparse canonical correlation analysis), and integrative models for predicting clinical outcomes._ |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _Advanced multivariate machine learning models and large-scale meta-analyses successfully mitigate the heavy burden of multiple statistical testing while boosting signal detection power. Through these methods, researchers have reliably identified hundreds of genetic loci that significantly influence brain structure and drive neurodegenerative disease progression._ |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _The field is severely constrained by the "high-dimensionality-small-sample-size" problem. This dramatically increases the risk of model overfitting, false discoveries, and challenges in generalizing models across diverse populations._ |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _Future research must focus on adapting robust deep learning techniques to handle multi-omics data and creating scalable, cloud-based tools for decentralized consortium data. Additionally, there is a pressing need for novel machine learning strategies designed specifically to process the high dimensionality of whole-genome and whole-exome sequencing data._ |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _The multivariate analysis and machine learning strategies detailed in this review can be directly adapted to our genome sequencing pipelines to effectively link discovered genetic variants with complex clinical phenotypes and disease outcomes._ |
