## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _Fisheries science relies on massive, resource-intensive data collection and analysis to monitor and manage marine resources. Machine learning (ML) is needed to automate and improve the understanding of complex marine ecosystem dynamics._ |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _The paper comprehensively reviews ML applications in fisheries science, organizing them across hierarchical scales from genomics and individual biometrics to fleet behavior and ecosystem dynamics. The study examines both shallow and deep learning techniques—such as CNNs, RF, and Bayesian networks—evaluating their operationality and robustness for various tasks._ |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _ML algorithms significantly accelerate data processing, such as using CNNs to rapidly map environmental DNA (eDNA) sequences to taxonomic groups and automating onboard catch monitoring via computer vision. In genomics, models like RF successfully handle multicollinearity and high-dimensional data to isolate discriminative SNPs for population assignment._ |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _Few limitations identified from this paper were: (i) ML implementations suffer from "black-box" opacity, vulnerability to adversarial attacks or domain shifts, and a heavy reliance on high-quality, unbiased training data. (ii)Operational challenges like camera occlusion, highly variable lighting, and ethical privacy concerns regarding crew surveillance also hinder real-world deployment._ |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _Future efforts must prioritize "trustworthy AI" by standardizing working environments, anonymizing confidential data, and mitigating algorithmic biases to maintain stakeholder trust._ |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _The paper's insights on applying ML techniques, specifically Random Forests and deep learning, to handle high-dimensional SNP data and accelerate eDNA taxonomic classification directly informs our genome sequencing and marker identification pipelines._ |
