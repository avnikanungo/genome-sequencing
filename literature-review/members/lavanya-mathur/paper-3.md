# 📄 Paper 3: An integrated encyclopedia of DNA elements in the human genome
*   **Journal / Conference**: Nature (2012)
*   **DOI**: `10.1038/nature11247`
*   **Link**: [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature11247)

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | The vast majority of the human genome does not code for proteins, and prior to this study the function of most of these non-coding regions remained unknown. This paper addresses that gap by systematically mapping which regions of the genome show biochemical activity, aiming to assign function beyond the well-studied protein-coding regions. |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The ENCODE Consortium generated and integrated 1,640 datasets across 147 human cell types using a wide range of experimental assays, including RNA sequencing, ChIP-seq for transcription factor binding, DNase-seq for chromatin accessibility, histone modification profiling, and DNA methylation analysis. Results from these diverse assays were standardized and combined to classify the genome into functional categories such as promoters, enhancers, and repressed regions. |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The study found that 80.4% of the human genome shows biochemical activity in at least one cell type, identifying approximately 400,000 enhancer-like regions and 70,000 promoter-like regions. Non-coding genetic variants linked to disease through genome-wide association studies were found to be significantly enriched within these newly annotated functional regions, often corresponding to specific, disease-relevant cell types. |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | The study sampled only a limited number of cell types and transcription factors relative to the full diversity present in the human body, meaning the functional map remains incomplete. Additionally, detecting biochemical activity at a region does not necessarily confirm that the activity has a meaningful biological function, a distinction the authors acknowledge and that was later debated in the scientific community. |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The authors recommend expanding this work to additional cell types, transcription factors, and histone modifications to build a more complete functional map of the genome. They also suggest that combining this functional annotation with individual genome sequencing could eventually help researchers interpret the impact of specific genetic variants on human health and disease. |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | This paper provides the foundational functional annotation data (transcription factor binding, chromatin accessibility, histone marks) that many later deep learning genomics models, such as DeepSEA and Basset, were directly trained to predict from raw DNA sequence. |
